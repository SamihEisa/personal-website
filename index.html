Research

My work lies at the intersection of computer science, digital health, distributed systems, and trustworthy intelligent technologies. I am particularly interested in how continuously evolving digital twins can remain synchronized with the systems or people they represent, while providing credible and interpretable support for human decision-making.

  • Trustworthy adaptive digital twins: continuous updating, synchronization, model validity, and lifecycle trustworthiness.
  • Healthcare and Virtual Human Twins: patient-centred models for monitoring, gait analysis, remote assessment, and clinical decision support.
  • Credibility and uncertainty: verification, validation, uncertainty quantification, confidence communication, and model reliability.
  • Secure digital infrastructures: IoT trust, distributed systems, blockchain-enabled data sharing, provenance, and auditable updates.

About

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Minho in 2018, where I developed data-driven methods for modelling behavioural patterns in Ambient Assisted Living environments. My research career has since developed through positions at INESC-ID, Centro de Computação Gráfica, Centro Algoritmi, and academic institutions in Portugal, Malaysia, and Sudan.

Although my work has crossed several domains, a common thread connects it: the creation of digital representations of real-world entities that can support monitoring, trust, and decision-making. This trajectory now converges toward trustworthy adaptive digital twins and human-centred intelligent systems.

Research Vision

My long-term vision is to contribute to the scientific foundations of digital twins that evolve continuously while remaining trustworthy. I am especially interested in how such systems can assess their own validity, communicate uncertainty, and support human decisions in domains where reliability matters.

In healthcare, this vision points toward Virtual Human Twins that integrate patient data, computational models, and trustworthy AI-assisted reasoning to support personalized, preventive, and clinically meaningful decision-making.
