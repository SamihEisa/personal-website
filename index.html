Research

My research focuses on how digital representations of patients can be created, maintained, validated, and trusted throughout their lifecycle. I am particularly interested in the mechanisms that enable healthcare digital twins to remain synchronized with real-world patient data while supporting reliable and clinically meaningful decisions.

Research Themes

  • Trustworthy Healthcare Digital Twins: Patient-centric digital twins, adaptive updating, synchronization mechanisms, and longitudinal patient modelling.
  • Model Credibility and Validation: Verification, validation, uncertainty quantification (VVUQ), runtime monitoring, and credibility assessment of digital twin systems.
  • Healthcare Monitoring and Behavioural Modelling: Gait analysis, remote patient monitoring, Ambient Assisted Living, and sensor-based assessment of health conditions.
  • Secure and Distributed Digital Infrastructures: Distributed systems, IoT trust, blockchain technologies, privacy-preserving architectures, and dependable data exchange.

About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at INESC-ID, University of Lisbon. My research career has evolved through healthcare technologies, Ambient Assisted Living, distributed systems, computer vision, IoT security, and blockchain-enabled infrastructures. Although these topics appear diverse, they share a common objective: understanding how reliable digital representations of real-world entities can be created and maintained. This trajectory has naturally converged toward healthcare digital twins, where sensing, modelling, synchronization, trust, and validation must be integrated into coherent patient-centred systems. I received my PhD in Computer Science from the University of Minho, where I developed data-driven methods for modelling behavioural patterns of elderly individuals living independently using sensor technologies.

Research Vision

My long-term vision is to contribute to the scientific foundations required for the safe adoption of healthcare digital twins in clinical practice. I am particularly interested in developing trustworthy digital twins that continuously evolve with patient data, provide transparent and credible predictions, and support personalized healthcare interventions. To achieve this, I investigate the intersection of healthcare sensing, distributed systems, formal modelling, verification, and intelligent adaptive systems.
